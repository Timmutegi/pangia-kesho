FAQS

1. What is Pangia Kesho?

Pangia Kesho is a unique insurance product for church members offering an in-patient benefit, accidental death cover, accidental total permanent disability cover and a funeral cover.


2. How do you enrol for cover?

You enrol for the cover by clicking on the "apply now" button on this website. You will then be required to fill in all the fields provided on the form.


3. What is the minimum premium that one can pay for the minimum benefit of this product?

The minimum premium is Kshs. 3800 per annum for one individual. This gives an inpatient benefit of Kshs. 200,000 annually.


4. Can the product offer an outpatient?

Pangia Kesho covers inpatient only but in case there is need for an outpatient then Britam can offer a different product for this..


5. What is the age limit for taking this policy?

On joining adults should be between 18-65 years but renewal is up to 70 years.


6. What are the mandatory documents required to enroll for this cover?

A copy of identification documents both yours and your spouse (if applicable) and passport size photographs of all persons to be under the cover.


7. Are there waiting periods for this cover?

Standard waiting periods apply such as 1 (one) month for all illnesses and deaths, 9 (nine) months for maternity. Detailed waiting periods are as indicated below:

Natural Death 1 Month
Maternity and pregnancy related conditions 9 Months
Surgery 12 Months

8. Are clients allowed to visit any of the hospitals in Kenya?

One can visit one of the over 400 hospitals countrywide in the Britam Medical Provider's Listing.


9. Can I (a member of a group) cover my brother, sister and parents who are my "dependents"?

A member is allowed to include members of his/her nuclear family but at an additional cost.


10. What documents are required to process Last expense claim?

  • A duly filled claim form
  • Certified copy of ID (Birth certificate for children and dependants).
  • ID copy of claimant.
  • If death as a result of an accident, a police abstract should also be attached for death claims.
